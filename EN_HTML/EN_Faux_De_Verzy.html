A strange forest in the heart of Champagne-Ardennes.

The Verzy Faux Forest is located in the middle of the Reims mountain in the Champagne-Ardennes department. This forest contains more than 800 faux trees, which are twisted beeches, a type of beech tree with twisted branches and twigs that droop back on themselves, giving them the appearance of an umbrella. Some trees can grow up to a maximum of 5 meters tall

This forest has received the label of exceptional forest, aiming to protect France's forest heritage.

In winter, the Verzy Faux trees reveal more random shapes, showing bare, curved trunks and branches without the foliage that appears in other seasons. In the summer, the trees resemble small huts, forming very dense and voluptuous umbrellas. In the autumn, beautiful orange-red colors can be found, which can create beautiful effects for photos.

The existence of these trees remains a mystery, although several theories are being considered, the most probable being that of genetic mutation. These trees are very rare, and only exist in a few places in the world, in Germany (Süntel massif) and Sweden (in the county of Scania). In fact, twisted beeches are a species of tree with very low fertility (germination rate lower than 10%), and have an approximately 350-year life expectancy. To preserve this species, the natural environment must be preserved. The tourist site of Verzy Faux receives a few thousand visitors per year, threatening local biodiversity and wildlife. That is why the site has put in place a marked path that only allows access to about ten twisted beeches. This approach to preserving the environment is also there to allow the beauty of flora and fauna to be passed on to future generations
