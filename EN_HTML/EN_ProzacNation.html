Prozac nation a touching book by the emotions that makes us to feel

Prosac nation is an autobiographical book written by Elizabeth Wurtzel in 1994 retracing his life from his childhood to his youthful life woman, Elizabeth is suffering from depression, we manage to hang on to the character through the inner dialogues she can have with herself. Elizabeth tells us about her past, starting with her family problems, the divorce of his parents, the strained relationship between them and the impact it may have had on her. His father shows total indifference towards her when her mother is overprotective.Despite her depression beginning at age 10/12, she was gifted for studies and was able to integrate Harvard a school prestigious in the United States, we follow his journey within his school.

She tells us how she discovered drugs, alcohol and sexual debauchery. The book makes us feel strong emotions by the naturalness of the situations he experiences in which one can make parallels with our life, toxic and deeply rooted human relationships in the present. His mental distress is accentuated by taking self-awareness and the highlighting of one's own faults, the looking for the slightest attention his psychological change as he as the book helps to better understand the effects of depression on a person and allow to speak about this subject freely without taboo. Elizabeth Wurtzel was one of the first to be one of the first patients to be prescribed antidepressants bearing the name Prozac.
