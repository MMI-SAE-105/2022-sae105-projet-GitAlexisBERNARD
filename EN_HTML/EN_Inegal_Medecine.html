Unequal access to medicine

Artificial intelligence surrounds us, but remains complex in its use. To summarize, AI refers to artificial intelligence, which exists in two forms: weak AI, which surrounds us and can execute lists of instructions, called algorithms, and acts as if it were intelligent, but is not really; and strong AI, which does not yet exist, but whose goal is to be able to learn and improve on its own and become autonomous. These artificial intelligences, through their complexity and methods (machine learning), will greatly aid medicine

Currently, AI seek to reproduce human medical activities, seeking to facilitate the tasks of doctors such as X-ray image analysis to detect tumors by scanner. In the future, this technology is planned in the prediction of a disease and its evolution and in the recommendation of treatment personalized and in decision support allowing the doctor to best target the potential illnesses of a patient.Artificial intelligence is making great strides technologies in medicine but still remains fairly in its infancy because the use of programs IT affecting human beings directly or indirectly and highly regulated everything related to it requires very complex procedures that can lead to long waiting times for a future release to the general public

AI is already starting to revolutionize the world in other areas such as creation synthetic image, it is from artificial intelligence like midday. a tool very very powerful but poses human moral problems because it goes against human principles, this can also be the case for artificial intelligence in the case of medicine strong ai can they be only intended to save us? but for the moment weak computer programs are preferred
